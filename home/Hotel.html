El Rancho

The Historic El Rancho Hotel is a legendary Route 66 landmark in Gallup, known as the "Home of the Movie Stars". Opened in 1936 by the brother of film director D.W. Griffith, it served as a base for Hollywood casts and crews filming Westerns in the surrounding desert. Today, it is a National Historic Landmark that retains its rustic, "Old West" charm through its grand lobby, split-log stairways, and massive stone fireplace.

Hotel Room's

Ronald Reagan

Who's Ronald Reagan? Ronald Reagan stayed at this legendary Route 66 hotel while filming Westerns in the 1930s and 40s. While Ronald Reagan stayed at the El Rancho Hotel several times, his most notable filming experiences in the Gallup area were for the Western movie The Bad Man (1941) and episodes of the television series Death Valley Days. You can stay in Room 103, which is the exact suite Reagan occupied. It is described as a large, nostalgic space with original features.






John Wayne

John Wayne was a frequent guest at the El Rancho Hotel in Gallup while filming legendary Westerns like Fort Apache (1948) and She Wore a Yellow Ribbon (1949). A popular piece of local lore at the El Rancho claims that John Wayne once rode a horse directly into the 49er Lounge to order a beer, a story often shared by staff and displayed in the hotel's lobby exhibits.






Lucille Ball

Lucille Ball was another legendary guest of the Historic El Rancho Hotel in Gallup, New Mexico. Like Reagan and Wayne, she stayed there during Hollywood's "Golden Age" when the area was a major production hub for Western films.
